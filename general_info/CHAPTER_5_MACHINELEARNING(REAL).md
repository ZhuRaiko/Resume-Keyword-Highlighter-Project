## 5.1 Overview

SkillHighlight is a context-aware résumé analysis system that combines pretrained sentence embeddings with a K‑nearest neighbors (KNN) classifier and linguistic validation to highlight important competency statements and compute a "self‑promotion" score for each sentence.

Key components
- Embeddings: SentenceTransformer `all‑MiniLM‑L6‑v2` (384 dimensions).
- Classifier: scikit‑learn `KNeighborsClassifier` (k = 5) trained on labeled résumé sentences.
- Keyword validation: spaCy-based heuristics and context rules.
- Interface: Streamlit web UI for demonstration and manual inspection.

Summary of principal quantitative findings (report these exactly in thesis):
- KNN classifier (held-out test): Accuracy = 86.2%; Precision = 97.1%; Recall = 92.3%; F1 = 94.6%.
- Keyword highlighting: Precision = 97.1%; F1 = 94.6%.
- End-to-end scoring (hybrid KNN + heuristics): Accuracy = 90.0% with mean score separation ≈ 0.690 between high- and low-quality sentences.
- Cross-validation: Mean F1 = 0.817 ± 0.121.

These results support the claim that combining semantic embeddings with lightweight KNN classification and context-aware filtering produces robust detection of self-promotion and reliable keyword highlighting for résumé content.

## 5.3 Methods (concise, thesis-style)

5.3.1 Embedding and representation
- Each sentence is encoded into a 384‑dim vector using a pretrained SentenceTransformer. Mean pooling over token vectors produces sentence embeddings.
- Embeddings are L2-normalized when using cosine-based nearest-neighbour comparisons.

5.3.2 KNN classification
- A KNN classifier (k=5) is trained on the labeled embedding vectors to predict whether a sentence is self-promoting.
- The KNN positive-class probability is computed as the fraction of positive labels among the k nearest neighbors.

5.3.3 Heuristic scoring
- The hybrid score combines KNN probability with additive heuristics: checks for numeric metrics, achievement verbs, bullet formatting, sentiment polarity, action verbs, and short-sentence penalties. The result is clamped to [0,1].

5.3.4 Keyword highlighting
- A spaCy-based pipeline applies lemma matching and context rules (POS, dependency checks) to avoid false positives (e.g., dates, unrelated noun phrases) and annotate spans with labels for rendering.

### 5.4.4 Embedding visualization and qualitative analysis

To explore the geometric structure that underlies the KNN classifier, we project sentence embeddings into two dimensions using complementary dimensionality‑reduction methods. Principal Component Analysis (PCA) on the full embedding set (`model_evaluation/bert_pca.png`) provides a fast, global view: the projection shows a broad gradient and two main lobes in which positive (self‑promotional) and negative sentences concentrate in different regions while still exhibiting a band of overlap between them. By contrast, the UMAP projection computed on a balanced subsample (`model_evaluation/bert_umap.png`) emphasises local neighborhood structure and reveals tighter, locally coherent clusters of semantically similar sentences; UMAP often exposes small islands of positive examples embedded in predominantly negative regions and vice versa.

Together these visualizations give a consistent picture. PCA demonstrates that the embedding space contains a large‑scale axis that correlates with self‑promotion content, which explains why a simple nearest‑neighbour classifier performs well overall. UMAP shows the finer structure that KNN exploits at prediction time: many positive examples sit in compact neighborhoods that support confident positive predictions, while ambiguous sentences lie in interleaved regions where neighbours have mixed labels. This combination—global separation with local pockets of overlap—accounts for the high precision observed in quantitative results and also motivates the use of small, human‑interpretable heuristics to resolve borderline cases.

Closing discussion: the embedding plots validate the design choice of pairing pretrained sentence embeddings with a nearest‑neighbour classifier. They show sufficient class structure to justify KNN as a lightweight, interpretable classifier in this setting, while the visible areas of label intermixing highlight the practical limits of purely geometric decision rules and motivate hybrid scoring (KNN probability plus context heuristics) and qualitative inspection via nearest‑neighbour examples.

## 5.5 Discussion

- The combined approach leverages semantic similarity for generalization (BERT embeddings) and simple, human‑interpretable heuristics for clarity and feedback. High precision shows the system is conservative in labelling positive claims, which is desirable for résumé scoring where false positives could mislead reviewers.
- Score calibration: because heuristics are additive, calibration on a validation set is important — report chosen thresholds and their impact on precision/recall when using scores for binary decisions.
- Failure modes: near-duplicate phrasing with different contexts, ambiguous short fragments, and noisy extraction from PDFs can cause errors; nearest-neighbour examples illustrate these.

---

## 5.6 Limitations and threats to validity

- Domain specificity: training on student or local resumes reduces immediate generalizability to other fields or countries. Evaluate with domain-specific datasets before deployment.
- Annotation noise: if labels were produced by a small number of annotators, inter-annotator variability may affect reported metrics.
- Heuristic bias: manually tuned bonuses can advantage certain writing styles; report values and perform sensitivity analysis.
- Visualization limits: 2D embeddings are qualitative and should not be overinterpreted.

---

## 5.7 Recommendations and practical next steps

- For scale: replace exact KNN with approximate NN (Faiss, hnswlib) if training corpus grows significantly.
- For accuracy: try a small supervised head on frozen embeddings (logistic regression or an MLP) and compare to KNN.
- For robustness: add OCR preprocessing for scanned resumes and extend evaluation to multilingual corpora.
- For fairness: analyze biases across demographic slices and include fairness metrics in future reporting.
